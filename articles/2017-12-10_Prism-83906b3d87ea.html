Overview

Prism is a customer research platform which uses surveys as well as human and bot-moderated interviews to capture customer feedback, opinions and attitudes to help companies form a complete picture of their customers.

Why now?

We envision a world where the pace of learning for marketing and product teams is the single greatest source of long-term competitive advantage.

In today’s environment, competitive moats can come in many forms, through technology advantages, network effects, brand loyalty etc.

Our assertion is that continuously staying “close” to customers will be the single greatest competitive advantage that a company can have when all these other factors can be quickly replicated or made redundant.

Market & Background

Prism is at the intersection of the large existing Market Research Market ($44B in 2016) and fast growing Customer Experience Market ($16.91B by 2022)*.

Traditionally, companies will hire large and expensive market research consultants to perform customer research or manage it in-house. The third alternative is “non-consumption”, which is the biggest opportunity that presents itself to us in expanding the market.

Founding Team

The founding team comes from the Product & Growth team at Visual.ly where they helped grow the marketplace there from founding to $5M in ARR in under 3 years and were responsible for generating and qualifying the thousands of monthly leads coming from content marketing into MQL’s based on psychographic profiling using marketing automation.

Business Model & Strategy:

Our approach to addressing the problems of the existing customer research methods boil down to the following dimensions:

  1. Speed/Efficiency — Traditional customer research done by companies typically around a new product or feature release. Since it typically takes lot’s of manual work from internal resources and teams, companies resort to doing it every 3–6 months on average. Prism’s aim is to improve this by an order of magnitude to enable continuous customer research.
  2. User Experience — Traditional customer research involves a patchwork process of different tools and methods for collecting qualitative customer feedback. Prism aims to simplify the user experience for both the respondents by enabling a more conversational-style interface and for the companies by providing a centralized dashboard to collect and analyze all of the information collected.

Our Go-to market approach is to focus in on a few killer use-cases, like helping companies understand churn via customer exit interviews, in order to differentiate ourselves from the other players in the space.

Competition

The competitive landscape for customer research tools in 2017 has a many tools for traditional surveying (survey gizmo, survey monkey just to name a few), point-solutions around specific use-cases like customer success (e.g: medallia, gainsight etc.), however, there are no single source of record for all customer insights that automated and comes from a mobile-first design ethos.

  • Since opening up our private beta in March of 2017, we’ve brought in $35,000 in revenue from 24 paying customers.

Technology

The prism platform is made up of the following components.

  1. Sourcing engine — Our user data acquisition tool (UDG) allows us to find nearly any persona online and contact them to participate in a study at minimal marginal cost. This capability is unique in our market and allows us to offer our customer recruiting offering for ‘new audience’ members. For getting feedback from existing users/customers, we’ve built custom webhook integrations that are event-based (e.g: email churned customers an exit survey automatically) and outreach email templates that improve response rates up to 10x (10% vs 1% companies typically get).
  2. Scheduler — One of the most time-consuming aspects of conducting customer research is scheduling time to speak, which we’ve automated through our platform for moderated interviews and are in the process of eliminating the need for by using more asynchronous chat-bot based interviews and survey forms.
  3. Interview automation — Starting with interview script templates, customers can manage their own un-moderated surveys and interviews using our chatbot or web-based survey form, or leverage our professional researchers who use them as building blocks to conduct interviews with.

Moats and Long-Term Advantages

Our current and long term advantages come from the following:

  1. Distribution advantages — Our initial advantage comes from our ability to target potential customers as we scale using our in-house customer data acquisition tool (code name UDG) which uses publicly available data to let us reach out to and set up meetings with decision makers in product and marketing depts. Our secondary advantage comes from our background in generating leads via in-bound marketing with content, using content from our own research studies as content to fuel this flywheel.
  2. Two-sided network effects — the more researchers we add to the platform the more potential value there is to customers looking for solutions. Likewise, the more companies on the platform, the more appealing it is for new researchers to join because of the variety of the work and the consistency in pay.
  3. Data network effects — As we scale, the more unique 1st party customer data we collect on users preferences, wants and needs, the more we will be able to benchmark and make stronger product/mktg related suggestions for improvements. By first cataloging our own customer’s users responses to surveys and interviews, we’ll be able to build up a large network/panel of participants for future customer research without paying any supply-side acquisition costs.

Deal Positives

  1. Experienced founding team with domain expertise and previous exit.
  2. Proven product development execution and early revenue traction.
  3. High-capital efficiency. Low burn-rate.

Deal Concerns

  1. Single-founder teams are not typical.
  2. Team is fully remote and we intent to keep a large % of the company outside the bay area.
  3. Market is still early and unclear how it will evolve.

Summary

In summary, we believe that as the market continues to evolve, we will be able to play a critical role in helping companies create empathy for their customers to help them build more successful products and market more effectively.

Our long-term competitive advantage will come from our ability to help our customers stay close to their customers by leveraging the data and insights that we gather to make actionable product and audience recommendations.
